Opportunity Rover

NASA's Opportunity rover was one of the most successful and enduring interplanetary missions. Opportunity landed on Mars in early 2004 soon after its twin rover Spirit. Opportunity operated almost 15 years, setting several records and making a number of key discoveries.

  • Opportunity and its twin Spirit were tasked with studying sites on Mars where conditions may once have been favorable for life.
  • Opportunity found evidence that Mars may once have been able to sustain microbial life.
  • Opportunity exceeded its life expectancy by 60 times and had traveled more than 28 miles (45 kilometers) by the time it reached its appropriate final resting spot on Mars â€“ Perseverance Valley.
  • Opportunity stopped communicating with Earth after a severe Mars-wide dust storm blanketed its location in June 2018.
